The Future of Local-First Software

local-first-future.mp3 1h 24m May 30, 2026

Summary

The Rundown: Martin Kleppmann discusses the principles behind local-first software and why CRDTs are becoming practical for real-world applications beyond simple text editing.

The Details:

  • Local-first means the app works offline by default, syncing when connectivity returns
  • Automerge 2.0 achieves 100x performance improvement over the original, making CRDTs viable for documents with 500K+ operations
  • The "local-first" spectrum: from fully local (no server) to local-first with cloud sync — most apps should target the middle
  • Key challenge remains: access control and permissions without a central authority
  • Real adoption: Figma, Linear, and Notion all use CRDT-inspired approaches internally

Why It Matters: As AI tools generate more content locally, the need for offline-capable, sync-friendly architectures grows. Teams building desktop and mobile apps should evaluate local-first patterns now — the tooling has finally caught up with the theory.
